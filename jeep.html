Jeep Air

Challenge

We were tasked by Ken Carbone, founding partner of Carbone Smolan Agency, to utilize of his famous Unify, Simplify, Amplify methodology to extended a legacy brand into a new arena of business. The project being academic in nature, my team and I decided to choose Jeep-- doing a deep historical business and brand audit in order to uncover what new market Jeep would be best fit to enter.

Strategic Insights

The findings from our audit led us to a number of clues as to which direction Jeep could take in a new sector. Taking a look at Jeep's history, we found that Jeep was the first mass production four-wheel drive vehicle and that it was originally sold to the military-- and later to farmers as a vehicle that can get to hard to reach places, and finally as a family vehicle. Seeing this proliferation of progression from military to consumer vehicle-- tied to the philosophy of a pride in ruggedness and ability to get to places others' can't-- we pinpointed a market that was copasetic with Jeep's existing brand narrative and business model.

Results:

  • A provision of Jeep's strategic positioning within a comprehensive analysis of the current and future drone market.
  • A complete visual identity system and product extension brand guidelines
  • A business strategy explaining how to enter the market in a successful and timely manner
  • A cohesive launch strategy
  • Conceptual design of how Jeep Air could fit into Jeep's existing brand platform
Off-roading:

Of all groups that participated, Jeep Air's Unify, Simplify, Amplify exercise was selected and presented in its entirety at HOW Design Live 2016 by Ken Carbone.
